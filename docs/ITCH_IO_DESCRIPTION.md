# itch.io Description Draft

> A simple little Flappy Bird clone.
> Or is it?

## Short Description

Flappy Something begins as a shamelessly cheap bird game: bright ads, impossible promises, and a Gate 40 nobody can cross. Following a disputed high score leads you through an obsolete phone, erased company records, and a lost version called Skyline 256. It is a story about preservation, the people behind a game, and whether a game can remain playable after support ends for everyone.

## More Than a High Score

At first, this is a High Score Chaser story: one impossible score, one suspicious player, and the stubborn urge to prove you can do better. But the chase slowly turns into something else. Every old device, missing record, and replaced game build asks the same question at the heart of Stop Killing Games: when support ends, should a game people once played and paid for simply become impossible to play?

Flappy Something does not ask for every live service to run forever. It asks what should remain when one ends: a playable build, a way to run it, and enough of its human story to understand why it mattered.

## How to Play

Click or tap inside **Flappy Something** to flap. Press **Space** for the same action; **Space** also starts a new run after a crash.

The game soon becomes an investigation conducted through the phone. Move between different apps, search for keywords, open messages, inspect records, and interact with anything that looks useful. The information you find is deliberately incomplete: collect clues from different places, keep important names and numbers in mind, and piece them together to uncover the larger story. Something that seems unimportant now may become the key to a later puzzle.

If you get stuck—or simply want to reach the later parts without solving every step slowly—open the **Terminal** app on the phone's Home screen. It contains a chapter-by-chapter guide explaining the current objective and every step needed to continue.

If the investigation still does not make sense, use the walkthrough video provided on this page. You can follow it as a solution guide, or simply watch the complete playthrough instead of playing the game yourself.

Later, the flight becomes more deliberate: collect every glowing route point in one run before Gate 40. Missing one keeps the route sealed, but the pattern never changes—each attempt is there to teach you something.

## Taking a Break

Progress is stored locally in your browser through two save systems:

- **Automatic checkpoint:** the game automatically updates your save whenever you enter a new chapter. Individual clues do not create separate checkpoints.
- **Manual save:** open **FileBox** on the phone's Home screen and choose **Save progress here** to preserve the current chapter in a separate slot. You can return to FileBox later and choose **Load manual save**.

FileBox also lets you load the latest automatic checkpoint, restart the current chapter, or restart the entire investigation. Saves remain on the same browser and device, but may be lost if you clear the site's stored data or switch browsers.

## Developer Tools / Chapter Preview

Want to revisit a moment or quickly inspect a later chapter? Press **Ctrl + Shift + D**, or launch the game with `?debug=true` in the URL. The Developer Debug Mode provides direct previews for Chapters 1–10 and opens the relevant phone app for each one.

These chapter snapshots are intended for testing and exploration. Selecting one replaces the current session's story progress, so use them when you want to browse, not when you are preserving a first playthrough.
